Associate Professor in the Philosophy Department at the College of the Holy Cross, I studied for my Ph.D (and M. Phil. and M.A.) in the Philosophy Department at Columbia University, in New York City, and for my Laurea in the Philosophy Department at the Universit√† degli studi di Firenze, in Florence, Italy. My research focuses on issues in the general areas of Metaphysics, Philosophy of Biology, and the Philosophy of Food.

Here is a copy of my academic CV. For the academic year 2015-2016, I will be directing the Natural World Cluster of the Montserrat Program. My work in progress includes a volume with Elena Casetta titled The Metaphysics of Life Sciences, for Bloomsbury Academic. Since my Laurea dissertation I have been fascinated by the philosophy of David Lewis and I am finishing up a short book (~35-40,000 words) in Italian on the topic, for Carocci editore. Over the past five years I have been accumulating material for a monography on the philosophy of food and preparing several pieces on this up and coming philosophy branch.

For three years (2011-2014) I have been the guide to philosophy on About.com. Occasionally, I am happy to contribute philosophy articles for a general public to magazines and newspapers, especially articles on the philosophy of food.
